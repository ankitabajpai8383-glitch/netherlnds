About the Coast

A shoreline
born from
the sea itself

Scheveningen is the oldest seaside resort in the Netherlands and one of the premier beach destinations in Northern Europe. With 3.5 kilometres of clean sand, the iconic Kurhaus Grand Hotel, and a vibrant fishing harbour, this coastal neighbourhood of The Hague has drawn visitors since the 19th century.

What to do

Five essential
experiences

From the wild waves to the historic harbour, Scheveningen offers a richness of experience that rewards every kind of visitor.

01 / 05

The Grand Beach

3.5 kilometres of North Sea shore stretching from the iconic Kurhaus to the southern dunes. Swim, sun, and watch the kite surfers dance on the horizon.

Explore
02 / 05

The Pier

Walk 400 metres out over the North Sea on Scheveningen's famous pier. Four towers, a sea-view observation deck, and the thrill of open water beneath your feet.

Discover
03 / 05

Wind & Waves

Kite surfing, windsurfing, and surfing — Scheveningen's consistent North Sea winds make it one of the best wind sports destinations in the Benelux.

Experience
04 / 05

Dune Trails

The coastal dunes stretch north and south — a nature reserve of marram grass, sea buckthorn, and wildlife. Walk, cycle, or ride through a protected coastal landscape.

Wander
05 / 05

Harbour Village

The old fishing harbour still lands fresh catch daily. Eat raw herring from a stall, browse the fish market, or dine in one of the harbour-side restaurants at sunset.

Every season has its own character

July and August bring the full beach season — sunbathers, volleyball courts, beach bars, and sailing regattas. The Pier is alive with music from its terraces. Water temperatures reach a pleasant 18°C. Book accommodations well in advance.

Getting
to Scheveningen

From Amsterdam's Schiphol Airport, Scheveningen is an easy 45-minute journey by train and tram. From central The Hague (Den Haag Centraal), tram line 1 runs directly to the beach in under 20 minutes.
